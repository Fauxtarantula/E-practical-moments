Experiment Protocol

We will describe the steps to be taken to complete the experiment.

Flowchart of Experiment

Step 1:
Take out the beam & knife edge from the science kit provided.

Step 2:
Place the beam on the knife edge to find the Center of Gravity (CG) of the beam.

Step 3:
Find the length L, from the center of the hole on the beam to the CG of the beam.

Step 4:
Step 4: Set up the apparatus shown on the website.

Step 5:
Ensure that the beam is horizontal.

Step 6:
Finding the value of Force F exerted on the loadcell.

Step 7:
Calculate and record down the value of 1/d.

Step 8:
Draw and record all your records for F, d & 1/d in a table.

Step 9:
We will be repeating steps 4 to 8 until you have records for at least 5 different values of d.

Step 10:
Now with records of 5 different values of d, on a piece of graph paper, plot a graph of F (vertical axis) against 1/d (horizontal axis) and draw a line of best fit through your plotted points.

Step 11:
Determine the gradient of the graph.

Step 12:
Calculate the weight of the beam by using the formula 'weight = gradient/L'.

Step 13:
Weigh the beam on the load cell.

Step 14:
Calculate the percentage error between the calculated and actual weight of the beam, using the formula ' [(Actual - Calculated)/Actual] x100% '.
